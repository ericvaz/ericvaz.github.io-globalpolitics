Unit 1

Introduction to Sociology and Global Societal Structures

Welcome to the first unit of our course, "Introduction to Sociology and Global Societal Structures." As we embark on this journey to understand the global order and future dynamics, it is essential to grasp the fundamentals of society and the global structures that underpin it. In this unit, we will explore key sociological concepts and theories that provide a framework for understanding the social world. These include an examination of social structures, institutions, cultural practices, and processes of social change. We will delve into how societies are organized, how they develop, and the factors that influence their change over time.

We will also begin our discussion of global societal structures, including the basics of international relations and geopolitical forces. By understanding the foundations of international society, we can better appreciate the forces shaping our globalized world. Our key reading for this unit, "Sociology: A Global Introduction" by John J Macionis and Ken Plummer, offers an accessible and comprehensive introduction to the field of sociology, focusing on global and comparative perspectives.

By the end of this unit, you should have a solid grounding in sociological theory and global societal structures, forming a crucial foundation upon which the rest of this course will build. Remember, as you go through the material, to engage critically and think about how these broad concepts apply to specific situations and contexts in our modern world. We're excited to get started with this journey and are glad to have you on board. Now, let's start delving into the fascinating world of sociology and global societal structures!

Reading

"Sociology: A Global Introduction" by John J Macionis and Ken Plummer

Journal Articles

  1. "Globalization Theory: Lessons from the Exportation of McDonaldization and the New Means of Consumption" by George Ritzer (American Sociological Review). This article discusses globalization theory through the lens of the McDonaldization thesis and the new means of consumption.
  2. "The Globalization of Nothing" by George Ritzer (Social Thought & Research). This piece examines the process of globalization through the concept of "nothing," which includes phenomena like global credit cards and fast food chains that are centrally conceived and controlled.
  3. "The Three Waves of Globalization: Winds of Change in Professional, Institutional and Academic Genres" by Rosa Giménez Moreno (Revista de Lingüística y Lenguas Aplicadas). This article explores the three waves of globalization, providing a comprehensive historical context for understanding current global structures.

Videos

  1. "The Wisdom of Sociology: Sam Richards at TEDxLacador" Sam Richards, a renowned sociologist, discusses the value of sociological thinking and its application in understanding our social world. This TEDx talk is a great introduction to the field of sociology.
  2. What is Globalization?" - by The Globalization of World Politics. This video provides a concise and clear explanation of globalization, covering its economic, political, and cultural dimensions. It's an excellent introduction to the concept of globalization.
  3. "Sociology and the Social Sciences" - by The School of Life This video explores sociology and the social sciences more broadly, discussing their relevance and applicability in understanding human behavior and societal structures.
